Optimizing Kedah-Kelantan (KK) Cattle Weight Gain via Derivatives

Visualize the exact day a local Malaysian beef cattle breed hits its peak growth velocity using calculus.

🐄 The Bioscience Scenario

In Malaysian beef production, the indigenous Kedah-Kelantan (KK) cattle breed is highly valued for its heat tolerance and disease resistance. To optimize feed conversion ratios and predict marketing timelines, farmers must track the Average Daily Gain (ADG).

Instead of just measuring absolute weight, applying the first derivative $f'(x)$ allows us to calculate the exact rate of weight gain per day. This pinpoint accuracy tells production managers exactly when animal growth begins to slow down, signaling the ideal window for dietary adjustments or market scheduling.

🧮 Mathematical Modeling

Growth follows a realistic sigmoidal pattern, modeled here using a cubic polynomial tailored to Malaysian MARDI growth benchmarking data points (0 to 365 days post-weaning).

Cattle Weight Function $f(x)$
$f(x) = -0.000004x^3 + 0.0024x^2 + 0.15x + 110$

Yields the total weight in kilograms ($\text{kg}$) at day $x$.

Growth Velocity Function $f'(x)$
$f'(x) = -0.000012x^2 + 0.0048x + 0.15$

Calculates the instantaneous rate of change ($\text{kg/day}$) at exactly day $x$.
